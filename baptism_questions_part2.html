Part 2: Christian Living

1: What is the Christian standard for marriage? What is the only basis for a Scriptural divorce?

Matthew 19:4-6, 9 (NWT)
"Have you not read that the one who created them from the beginning made them male and female and said: 'For this reason a man will leave his father and his mother and will stick to his wife, and the two will be one flesh'? So that they are no longer two, but one flesh. Therefore, what God has yoked together, let no man put apart. . . . Whoever divorces his wife, except on the grounds of sexual immorality, and marries another commits adultery."
Explanation:
God's standard for marriage is one man and one woman united for life. Marriage is a sacred bond that should not be broken. The only Scriptural grounds for divorce is sexual immorality (adultery), which breaks the marriage covenant and gives the innocent mate the right to divorce and remarry.

2: Why should those living together as husband and wife be legally married? If you are married, are you sure that your marriage is legal and recognized by the government?

Titus 3:1 (NWT)
"Continue reminding them to be in subjection and to be obedient to governments and authorities."
Explanation:
Christians should obey governmental requirements, including legal marriage. Being legally married shows respect for authority and protects both partners legally and socially. It demonstrates that we honor both God's arrangement and human law.
Hebrews 13:4 (NWT)
"Let marriage be honorable among all, and let the marriage bed be without defilement, for God will judge sexually immoral people and adulterers."
Explanation:
Marriage should be held in honor and kept pure. Legal marriage protects the honor of the marriage arrangement and ensures that the intimate relationship is within God's approved bounds. It also provides legal protection and recognition for both spouses.

3: What is your role in the family?

For Children:
Listen to and obey parents, respecting both father and mother's instruction and discipline as part of God's arrangement for the family.
Proverbs 1:8 (NWT)
"Listen, my son, to the discipline of your father, and do not forsake the instruction of your mother."
For Husbands:
Exercise loving headship following Christ's example of self-sacrificing leadership. Headship is not domination but loving guidance and protection.
Ephesians 5:23, 25 (NWT)
"A husband is head of his wife just as the Christ is head of the congregation . . . Husbands, continue loving your wives, just as the Christ also loved the congregation."
For Fathers:
Discipline and teach children according to Jehovah's standards, avoiding harsh treatment that would discourage or embitter children.
Ephesians 6:4 (NWT)
"Fathers, do not be irritating your children, but go on bringing them up in the discipline and admonition of Jehovah."
For Children:
Obey parents in all things that are not contrary to God's law. This obedience pleases God and maintains proper order in the family.
Colossians 3:20 (NWT)
"You children, be obedient to your parents in everything, for this is well-pleasing to the Lord."
For Wives:
Respect husband's headship and support his leadership in the family. This is part of God's arrangement and contributes to family harmony and order.
1 Peter 3:1 (NWT)
"You wives, be in subjection to your husbands."

4: Why should we show respect for life?

Acts 17:25, 28 (NWT)
"[God] gives to all people life and breath and all things. . . . By him we have life and move and exist."
Explanation:
Life comes from God, and we depend on Him for our very existence. Since God is the source and sustainer of life, all life is sacred and should be respected and protected. We are accountable to Him for how we treat the gift of life.

5: Why should we not kill anyone, including an unborn baby?

Exodus 21:22, 23 (NWT)
"If men should struggle with each other and they hurt a pregnant woman and . . . if a fatality does occur, then you must give life for life."
Explanation:
God's law protected even unborn children, showing that life begins in the womb. Taking the life of an unborn child was treated as seriously as taking any other life. This shows God's view of the value of life at all stages of development.
Psalm 139:16 (NWT)
"Your eyes even saw me as an embryo; all its parts were written in your book regarding the days when they were formed, before any of them existed."
Explanation:
God knows and values each person even before birth, while they are still developing in the womb. This shows that unborn children are precious to God and should be protected. He has a plan for each life from conception.
Proverbs 6:16, 17 (NWT)
"Jehovah hates . . . hands that shed innocent blood."
Explanation:
God hates the shedding of innocent blood. This includes taking the life of unborn children, who are completely innocent and defenseless. Murder in any form is detestable to God and violates His law.

6: What is God's command regarding blood?

Acts 15:29 (NWT)
"Keep abstaining . . . from blood [and] from what is strangled."
Explanation:
Christians must abstain from blood in any form, whether eating it or receiving blood transfusions. This command shows respect for the sacredness of life, which blood represents. God views life as sacred and has restricted the use of blood.

7: Why must we love our Christian brothers and sisters?

John 13:34, 35 (NWT)
"I am giving you a new commandment, that you love one another; just as I have loved you, you also love one another. By this all will know that you are my disciples—if you have love among yourselves."
Explanation:
Love among Christians is the identifying mark of true disciples. Jesus commanded this love, and it serves as proof to others that we belong to him. Christian love should mirror the love Jesus showed—sacrificial, unselfish, and unconditional.

8: To avoid transmitting an infectious, potentially fatal disease to others: (a) Why should an infected person not initiate displays of affection, such as hugging and kissing? (b) Why should he not react negatively if some choose not to invite him into their home? (c) Why should a person who may have been exposed to an infectious disease voluntarily choose to have a blood test before beginning a courtship? (d) Why should a person with an infectious disease inform the coordinator of the body of elders before getting baptized?

Romans 13:8-10 (NWT)
"Do not owe anything to anyone except to love one another. . . . 'You must love your neighbor as yourself.' Love does not work evil to one's neighbor."
Explanation:
Love motivates us to protect others from harm. If we have an infectious disease, love requires us to take precautions to avoid spreading it to others, even if it means limiting physical contact or social activities. True love considers others' welfare above our own desires.
Philippians 2:4 (NWT)
"Look out not only for your own interests, but also for the interests of others."
Explanation:
Christians should consider how their actions affect others. This means being honest about health conditions and taking appropriate measures to protect fellow believers and the community. Such honesty and consideration demonstrate Christian love.

9: Why does Jehovah expect us to forgive others?

Colossians 3:13 (NWT)
"Continue putting up with one another and forgiving one another freely even if anyone has a cause for complaint against another. Just as Jehovah freely forgave you, you must also do the same."
Explanation:
We should forgive others because God has forgiven us. Since we have received God's undeserved mercy and forgiveness, we should extend the same kindness to others who wrong us. Forgiveness reflects God's love and mercy in our own lives.

10: What should you do if a brother slanders or defrauds you?

Matthew 18:15-17 (NWT)
"If your brother commits a sin, go and reveal his fault between you and him alone. If he listens to you, you have gained your brother. But if he does not listen, take along with you one or two more, so that on the testimony of two or three witnesses every matter may be established. If he does not listen to them, speak to the congregation. If he does not listen even to the congregation, let him be to you just as a man of the nations and as a tax collector."
Explanation:
Jesus outlined a progressive approach to resolving conflicts: first speak privately, then bring witnesses, then involve the congregation. This process aims to restore the relationship while maintaining justice and order. The goal is always reconciliation and restoring brotherly love.

11: How does Jehovah view the following sins?

Sins that prevent inheritance of God's Kingdom:
• Sexual immorality
• Use of images in worship (idolatry)
• Homosexuality
• Stealing
• Gambling (greediness)
• Drunkenness
1 Corinthians 6:9, 10 (NWT)
"Do not be misled. Those who are sexually immoral, idolaters, adulterers, men who submit to homosexual acts, men who practice homosexuality, thieves, greedy people, drunkards, revilers, and extortioners will not inherit God's Kingdom."
Explanation:
These practices are serious sins that prevent inheritance of God's Kingdom. God views them as incompatible with Christian living and requiring genuine repentance for forgiveness. They represent a rejection of God's standards and must be abandoned by those seeking to serve Him.

12: Regarding sexual immorality, which includes a variety of sexual acts outside of marriage, what are you determined to do?

1 Corinthians 6:18 (NWT)
"Flee from sexual immorality!"
Explanation:
Christians must actively avoid sexual immorality by fleeing from situations and temptations that could lead to such behavior. This requires decisive action and firm determination to maintain moral purity. We must not even get close to situations that could compromise our integrity.

13: Why should we abstain from the nonmedical use of addictive or mind-altering substances?

Romans 12:1, 2 (NWT)
"Present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God, a sacred service with your power of reason. And stop being molded by this system of things, but be transformed by making your mind over, so that you may prove to yourselves the good and acceptable and perfect will of God."
Explanation:
Our bodies and minds should be kept pure as a sacrifice to God. Drug abuse interferes with our ability to think clearly and make good decisions, preventing us from serving God with our full mental capacity. We need clear minds to understand and do God's will.

14: What are some of the demonic practices that God forbids?

Deuteronomy 18:10, 11 (NWT)
"There should not be found in you . . . anyone who employs divination, anyone practicing magic, anyone who looks for omens, a sorcerer, anyone binding others with a spell, anyone who consults a spirit medium or a fortune-teller, or anyone who inquires of the dead."
Explanation:
All forms of spiritism and occult practices are forbidden because they involve contact with demons. These practices are dangerous spiritually and draw people away from dependence on God. They include fortune-telling, magic, séances, and any attempt to communicate with the dead.

15: If a person commits a serious sin and wants to be restored to Jehovah's favor, what should he immediately do?

Psalm 32:5 (NWT)
"I confessed my sin to you; I did not cover my error. I said: 'I will confess my transgressions to Jehovah.'"
Explanation:
A person who has sinned seriously should confess to God and not try to hide their wrongdoing. This confession should be sincere and accompanied by genuine repentance. Covering sin only makes matters worse.
James 5:14, 15 (NWT)
"Is there anyone sick among you? Let him call the elders of the congregation to him, and let them pray over him, applying oil to him in the name of Jehovah. And the prayer of faith will make the sick one well, and Jehovah will raise him up. Also, if he has committed sins, he will be forgiven."
Explanation:
For serious sins, the person should also approach the congregation elders for spiritual help and guidance. The elders can provide counsel and prayer to help the person restore their relationship with God. They serve as spiritual physicians.

16: If you know that a fellow Christian has committed a serious sin, what should you do?

Leviticus 5:1 (NWT)
"If someone sins because he has heard a public call to testify and he is a witness or has seen or learned about it and he does not report it, then he will answer for his error."
Explanation:
If we know about serious sin in the congregation, we have a responsibility to report it to the elders. Failing to do so when we have knowledge makes us share in the guilt. This protects the congregation and helps the wrongdoer receive needed assistance.

17: If an announcement is made that someone is no longer one of Jehovah's Witnesses, how should we treat him?

1 Corinthians 5:11 (NWT)
"Stop keeping company with anyone called a brother who is sexually immoral or a greedy person or an idolater or a reviler or a drunkard or an extortioner, not even eating with such a man."
Explanation:
We should limit our association with disfellowshipped persons, not socializing or eating with them. This discipline aims to help them realize the seriousness of their actions and encourages repentance. It also protects the congregation's spiritual health.
2 John 10 (NWT)
"If anyone comes to you and does not bring this teaching, do not receive him into your homes or say a greeting to him."
Explanation:
We should not welcome those who have been removed from the congregation into our homes or give them friendly greetings, as this could undermine the discipline and seem to condone their behavior. The goal is to help them return to God.

18: Why should your close friends be those who love Jehovah?

Proverbs 13:20 (NWT)
"The one walking with the wise will become wise, but the one who has dealings with the stupid will fare badly."
Explanation:
Our close associates influence us significantly. Choosing friends who love God will help us grow in wisdom and spirituality, while bad associations can lead us away from God. We become like those we spend time with.
1 Corinthians 15:33 (NWT)
"Do not be misled. Bad associations spoil useful habits."
Explanation:
Bad associations gradually corrupt good character and habits. We need to be selective about our close friendships to protect our spiritual progress and moral standards. Good associations encourage us in righteous ways.

19: Why are Jehovah's Witnesses politically neutral?

John 17:16 (NWT)
"They are no part of the world, just as I [Jesus] am no part of the world."
Explanation:
Christians must remain separate from the world's political systems, following Jesus' example. Our citizenship is in God's Kingdom, not in human governments that will be replaced by God's rule. We cannot serve two masters.

20: Why should you obey the government?

Romans 13:1 (NWT)
"Let every person be in subjection to the superior authorities, for there is no authority except by God; the existing authorities stand placed in their relative positions by God."
Explanation:
God allows human governments to exist for maintaining order, so we should obey their laws as long as they don't conflict with God's laws. This shows respect for God's arrangement and helps maintain peace in society.

21: If there is a conflict between a human law and God's law, what would you do?

Acts 5:29 (NWT)
"We must obey God as ruler rather than men."
Explanation:
When human laws conflict with God's commands, we must choose to obey God. Our ultimate allegiance is to God, and His laws take precedence over human laws. We respectfully accept any consequences for this stand.

22: What scriptures can help you to remain separate from the world when choosing a job?

Micah 4:3 (NWT)
"Nation will not lift up sword against nation, nor will they learn war anymore."
Explanation:
Christians should avoid employment that involves military service or weapons manufacturing, as this conflicts with our commitment to peace and non-violence. We follow the principle of beating swords into plowshares.
Revelation 18:4 (NWT)
"Get out of her [Babylon the Great], my people, if you do not want to share with her in her sins, and if you do not want to receive part of her plagues."
Explanation:
We should avoid jobs that directly support false religion or other practices condemned by God, maintaining our separation from "Babylon the Great" (the world empire of false religion). This includes work in religious institutions that teach false doctrine.

23: What kind of entertainment and recreation will you choose, and what kind will you reject?

Psalm 11:5 (NWT)
"Jehovah . . . hates anyone who loves violence."
Explanation:
We should avoid entertainment that glorifies violence, since God hates violence and those who love it. Our entertainment choices should reflect godly values and not desensitize us to things God abhors.
Romans 12:9 (NWT)
"Abhor what is wicked; cling to what is good."
Explanation:
Christians should hate what is evil and hold fast to what is good. This principle guides our choices in entertainment, helping us select wholesome recreation that doesn't promote sin or corrupt thinking.
Philippians 4:8 (NWT)
"Whatever things are true, whatever things are of serious concern, whatever things are righteous, whatever things are chaste, whatever things are lovable, whatever things are well-spoken-of, whatever things are virtuous, and whatever things are praiseworthy, continue considering these things."
Explanation:
Our entertainment should meet high moral standards—being true, righteous, chaste, lovable, virtuous, and praiseworthy. This helps us focus our minds on uplifting content that builds us up spiritually rather than tearing us down.

24: Why do Jehovah's Witnesses not worship with other religious groups?

1 Corinthians 10:21 (NWT)
"You cannot be partaking of 'the table of Jehovah' and the table of demons."
Explanation:
We cannot participate in both true and false worship. Other religious groups practice false religion, so joining in their worship would compromise our exclusive devotion to Jehovah. True worship must be pure and undiluted.
2 Corinthians 6:17 (NWT)
"'Separate yourselves,' says Jehovah, 'and quit touching the unclean thing'; 'and I will take you in.'"
Explanation:
God commands His people to separate from unclean religious practices. Maintaining this separation ensures that God will accept us and that we remain spiritually clean. Religious mixing dilutes pure worship.

25: What principles will help you decide whether to participate in a celebration?

Psalm 106:35, 36 (NWT)
"They mingled with the nations and adopted their ways. They kept serving their idols, and these became a snare to them."
Explanation:
We should avoid adopting customs that have pagan or idolatrous origins, as these can become spiritual snares that draw us away from pure worship. We must carefully examine the roots of celebrations.
Ecclesiastes 9:5 (NWT)
"The dead know nothing at all."
Explanation:
Since the dead are unconscious, celebrations that honor or communicate with the dead are based on false beliefs and should be avoided. This includes holidays like Halloween and Day of the Dead.
John 17:16 (NWT)
"They are no part of the world, just as I am no part of the world."
Explanation:
Christians must remain separate from worldly celebrations that conflict with Biblical principles or promote values contrary to God's standards. Our celebrations should honor God, not the world.
1 Peter 4:3 (NWT)
"The time that has passed by is sufficient for you to have done the will of the nations when you carried on in acts of brazen conduct, unbridled passions, overdrinking, wild parties, drinking bouts, and lawless idolatries."
Explanation:
We should avoid celebrations characterized by excessive drinking, wild behavior, or other conduct that violates Christian principles. Our past worldly conduct should be left behind when we become Christians.

26: How do Bible examples help you decide whether to celebrate birthdays?

Genesis 40:20-22 (NWT)
"The third day was Pharaoh's birthday, and he made a feast for all his servants, and he brought out both the chief cupbearer and the chief baker in the presence of his servants. And he returned the chief cupbearer to his post of cupbearer . . . But he hanged the chief baker."
Explanation:
The Bible's only mention of birthday celebrations involves pagan rulers and ends with someone's execution. This negative association helps Christians understand that birthday celebrations are not practices that please God.
Matthew 14:6-8, 10 (NWT)
"When Herod's birthday was being celebrated, the daughter of Herodias danced for the occasion and pleased Herod so much that he promised with an oath to give her whatever she asked. Then she, at her mother's prompting, said: 'Give me here on a platter the head of John the Baptist.' So he sent and had John beheaded in the prison."
Explanation:
Another biblical birthday celebration resulted in the murder of John the Baptist. The consistent negative portrayal of birthday celebrations in Scripture guides Christians to avoid this practice. Both accounts involve violence and death.

27: Why do you want to follow the guidance of the elders?

Hebrews 13:17 (NWT)
"Be obedient to those who are taking the lead among you and be submissive, for they are keeping watch over you as those who will render an account, so that they may do this with joy and not with sighing, for this would be damaging to you."
Explanation:
Elders have been appointed to watch over our spiritual welfare and will answer to God for their care of the congregation. Following their guidance protects us spiritually and helps them fulfill their responsibility joyfully. They serve as shepherds under Christ.

28: Why is it important for you and your family to set aside time regularly to read and study the Bible?

Psalm 1:2, 3 (NWT)
"His delight is in the law of Jehovah, and he reads His law in an undertone day and night. He will be like a tree planted by streams of water, a tree that produces fruit in its season, the foliage of which does not wither. And everything he does will succeed."
Explanation:
Regular Bible reading brings spiritual nourishment and success. Like a well-watered tree that bears fruit, those who meditate on God's Word daily will flourish spiritually and be productive in God's service. God's Word provides the guidance we need for life.

29: Why do you love attending and participating in the meetings?

Psalm 22:22 (NWT)
"I will declare your name to my brothers; in the midst of the congregation I will praise you."
Explanation:
Meetings provide opportunities to praise God and share in worship with fellow believers. This corporate worship fulfills our desire to honor God publicly and encourage our brothers and sisters in the faith.
Hebrews 10:24, 25 (NWT)
"Let us consider one another so as to incite to love and fine works, not forsaking our meeting together, as some have the custom, but encouraging one another, and all the more so as you see the day drawing near."
Explanation:
Meetings help us encourage each other and motivate one another to love and good works. As the end draws near, this mutual encouragement becomes increasingly important for maintaining our faith and spiritual strength.

30: What is the most important work that Jesus has given us to do?

Matthew 28:19, 20 (NWT)
"Go, therefore, and make disciples of people of all the nations, baptizing them . . . , teaching them to observe all the things I have commanded you."
Explanation:
The most important work is making disciples—preaching the good news, helping people learn about God, and teaching them to follow Jesus' commands. This work is urgent and takes priority over other activities. It's our primary mission as Christians.

31: When we make donations to the Kingdom work or help our brothers and sisters, what attitude pleases Jehovah?

Proverbs 3:9 (NWT)
"Honor Jehovah with your valuable things."
Explanation:
We should give to honor God, offering our best and most valuable resources in service to Him. Giving should be motivated by love and respect for God, not by compulsion or for recognition from others.
2 Corinthians 9:7 (NWT)
"Let each one do just as he has resolved in his heart, not grudgingly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver."
Explanation:
God wants us to give cheerfully and willingly, not reluctantly or because we feel pressured. Our giving should come from the heart and be done with joy. The attitude behind our giving is more important than the amount.

32: What difficulties do Christians expect to experience?

Matthew 5:10-12 (NWT)
"Happy are those who have been persecuted for righteousness' sake, since the Kingdom of the heavens belongs to them. Happy are you when people reproach you and persecute you and lyingly say every sort of wicked thing against you for my sake. Rejoice and be overjoyed, since your reward is great in the heavens, for in that way they persecuted the prophets prior to you."
Explanation:
Christians should expect persecution and opposition for their faith. Rather than being discouraged, we can rejoice knowing that such experiences prove our faithfulness and will be rewarded by God. We follow in the footsteps of faithful servants before us.

33: Why is it a special privilege to be baptized as one of Jehovah's Witnesses?

Jeremiah 15:16 (NWT)
"Your word became to me the exultation and the rejoicing of my heart, for your name has been called on me, O Jehovah God."
Explanation:
Bearing God's name is a source of great joy and honor. Being baptized as one of Jehovah's Witnesses means we have the privilege of being called by God's name and representing Him to others. This is the greatest honor possible for any human.
